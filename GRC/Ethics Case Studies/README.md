# Ethics Case Studies

**Type:** Group papers (ITEC 450: Social and Professional Issues)

## Purpose

Two milestone papers examining professional ethics in computing — first surveying and comparing the field's codes of ethics, then applying ethical reasoning to a landmark security-vs-privacy dispute.

## Milestone 1 — Applying Ethical Frameworks and Codes of Ethics

A survey and cross-comparison of professional codes of ethics. It catalogs general codes (AMA, ABA, NASW, PMI, SPJ) and computing-specific ones (ACM, IEEE, ISACA, SANS, BCS), then compares five pairs (IEEE vs. ACM, ACM vs. BCS, ACM vs. AMA, ABA vs. AMA, PMI vs. IEEE) on emphasis, structure, and enforcement — highlighting how computing codes weigh societal impact, privacy, and data ethics.

## Milestone 2 — The FBI & Apple: Security vs. Privacy

An ethical analysis of the 2015 San Bernardino case, where the FBI sought to compel Apple to build a tool bypassing iPhone encryption. **My contribution:** the *Ethical Dilemma* section and the *Conclusion*, plus editing. My sections frame the core problem in security terms — that a "backdoor" can't be limited to one device and breaks the principles of Identity and Access Management (IAM) and Privileged Access Management (PAM) — and weigh the short-term investigative benefit against the long-term risk to millions of users. The paper analyzes the case through multiple ethical lenses (utilitarianism, social contract theory) and stakeholder perspectives.
