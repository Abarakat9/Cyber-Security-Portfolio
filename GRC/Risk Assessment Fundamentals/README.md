# Risk Assessment Fundamentals

**Type:** Written assignment (IT286) · **Framework:** NIST SP 800-30

## Purpose

An explanation of why risk assessment matters and what an organization should do after completing one, grounded in NIST risk-management guidance.

## Key topics

- **The five post-assessment actions** — Identify & Document, Analyze, Evaluate, Treat, and Monitor — framed as a repeatable risk-management cycle, including the point that not all risk can be eliminated and residual risk must be monitored.
- **Privacy-policy alignment** — a walkthrough of how Purdue's security policy applies the Notice / Choice / Access / Security / Enforcement model to post-assessment practice.
- **Key personnel & roles** — Risk Manager, IT/Security teams, employees/end users, and Compliance team, with the responsibilities each carries in the risk program.
- **Core definitions** — *threat* vs. *vulnerability* per NIST SP 800-30.
