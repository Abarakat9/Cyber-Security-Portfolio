# Business Impact Analysis (BIA) — Initech Technologies

**Type:** Written paper (ITEC 454) · **Framework:** NIST SP 800-34

## Purpose

A Business Impact Analysis for a fictional software firm, Initech Technologies, identifying its essential business functions and defining recovery objectives so the organization can prioritize what to restore first after a disruption.

## Key topics

- **Essential business functions** — Software Development, Client Support Services, Cloud Infrastructure, Internal IT/Network Admin, and Human Resources, each mapped to how it supports the organization's mission and revenue.
- **Recovery objectives** — RTO (Recovery Time Objective) and RPO (Recovery Point Objective) assigned per function, with a 1–5 priority ranking. Software Development and Cloud Infrastructure carry the tightest targets (8-hour RTO; 1–2 hour RPO) as the revenue-critical, data-sensitive functions; HR sits at the bottom (48-hour RTO).
- **Prioritization logic** — recovery order driven by operational criticality and data sensitivity, aligned to NIST SP 800-34 contingency-planning guidance.
